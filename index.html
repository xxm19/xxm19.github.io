Xiaomeng Xu 徐霄萌
xuxm at stanford dot edu

I am a third-year PhD candidate in Electrical Engineering at Stanford University, advised by Prof. Shuran Song and part of the REALab. I'm grateful to be supported by the Stanford Interdisciplinary Graduate Fellowship. Previously, I obtained a B.E. in Automation Engineering from Tsinghua University, where I worked with Prof. Li Yi and Prof. Leonidas J. Guibas (at Stanford).

My research focuses on leveraging machine learning algorithms to design and control robots, enabling them to perform complex manipulation tasks efficiently and robustly in the real world. Specifically, I'm interested in whole-body and mobile manipulation, whole-body and active sensing, and hardware software co-design.

Research

Mixture of Frames Policy: Multi-Frame Action Denoising for Bimanual Mobile Manipulation

Dian Wang*, Jisang Park*, Xiaomeng Xu, Han Zhang, Shuran Song†, Jeannette Bohg†
arXiv preprint, 2026
Website  •   Paper  •   Code


TL;DR: Bimanual mobile manipulation is inherently multi-frame: no single action frame is best across a long-horizon task. Mixture of Frames Policy dynamically leverages the most suitable frame throughout task execution.

PanoVine: Whole-Body Visuomotor Control for Soft Growing Vine Robot

Yimeng Qin*, Xiaomeng Xu*, William Heap, Aditi Oak, Shuran Song†, Allison Okamura†
arXiv preprint, 2026
Website  •   Paper  •   Code


TL;DR: Vine robots are soft, growing robots well suited to confined spaces. PanoVine is the first autonomous vine robot system, using rich whole-body vision feedback from 19 cameras distributed along its body to train an end-to-end visuomotor policy.

HoMMI: Learning Whole-Body Mobile Manipulation from Human Demonstrations

Xiaomeng Xu, Jisang Park, Han Zhang, Eric Cousineau, Aditya Bhat, Jose Barreiros, Dian Wang, Jeannette Bohg, Shuran Song
Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS 2026)
Embodied AI Workshop (CVPR 2026), Oral Presentation
Webpage  •   Paper  •   Video  •   Code


TL;DR: A data collection and policy learning framework that learns whole-body mobile manipulation and active perception directly from robot-free human demonstrations (Egocentric+UMI), without any teleoperation data.

Compliant Residual DAgger: Improving Real-World Contact-Rich Manipulation with Human Corrections

Human-to-Robot Workshop (CoRL 2025), Best Paper
Website  •   Paper  •   Code
